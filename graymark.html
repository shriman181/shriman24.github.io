How It Works

Four steps.
No disruption.

01
Ingest

Document Intake

Upload, API, email, or scan. Graymark accepts any format — PDF, photo, TIFF, degraded legacy scan — without requiring any change in applicant behaviour.

02
Extract

AI Extraction & Verification

Vision-language AI reads every document. Extracts structured fields. Cross-verifies against UIDAI, DigiLocker, land records, PFMS, and 40+ government APIs.

03
Route

Intelligent Workflow Routing

Applications route to the right department, in the right sequence, with SLA timers. No file sits unassigned. No handoff missed. Every step is fully accountable.

04
Decide

Human-Reviewed Decisions

Officers receive a pre-analysed file with AI recommendations. They verify and approve — in minutes, not days. Full audit trail for RTI, CAG, and legal compliance.

Architecture

Built for sovereign
infrastructure. Not
just the cloud.

Graymark deploys on NIC cloud, AWS GovCloud, Azure Government, or fully air-gapped on-premise. No citizen PII leaves your jurisdiction. MeitY data localisation requirements met from day one — not retrofitted.

The architecture is a translation layer. It reads your existing systems, makes them intelligent, and writes outputs in whatever format they already expect — COBOL, XML, REST, or fixed-width flat file.

"Government is not slow because it doesn't care. It's slow because nobody built the right infrastructure for it. That's what Graymark is."

Sarthak Arora founded Graymark with the conviction that the most consequential technology isn't built for the most glamorous industries — it's built for the ones that matter most. Before Graymark, he spent years studying the gap between policy intent and administrative execution in India's public sector: permits that stall for months over a missing signature, benefits that never reach eligible citizens, documents that accumulate in district offices because no system exists to read them.


Graymark is his answer to a question India's government has been asking for twenty years: how do you make an inherited system work at the speed of the people it serves?
